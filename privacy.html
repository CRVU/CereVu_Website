Privacy Policy

Effective Date: January 1, 2025
Last Updated: January 1, 2025
We are committed to protecting your privacy and health data

Introduction

CereVu Medical ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our CereVu technology, mobile application, website, and related services (collectively, the "Services").

This policy applies to all users of the Services, including patients, physicians, healthcare administrators, and website visitors. Please read this Privacy Policy carefully. By using our Services, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this policy.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Contact Information

We collect contact information to create and maintain your account, enable communication between patients and healthcare providers, and coordinate healthcare services:

  • Name: Used for account creation, personalization, and healthcare coordination
  • Email Address: For account authentication, communication, and important notifications
  • Phone Number: For account verification, two-factor authentication, and emergency contact
  • Physical Address: For healthcare coordination and medical device shipping

1.2 Health & Fitness Data

We collect comprehensive health data essential for our core monitoring functionality:

  • Biomarker data (vital signs, physiological measurements)
  • Pain assessment data and pain index measurements
  • Data from connected medical devices and sensors
  • Screening questionnaire responses
  • Continuous health monitoring data
  • Historical health trends and patterns
  • Cerebral oxygenation data (HbO₂)
  • Data quality and device adhesion status

1.3 Technical Information

We automatically collect certain technical information when you use our Services:

  • Device information (type, model, operating system)
  • IP address and browser type
  • Usage data (features accessed, session duration)
  • Device connectivity data (Bluetooth, USB)
  • Network activity logs for troubleshooting
  • Cookies and similar tracking technologies

1.4 Information You Provide

  • Registration and profile information
  • Communications with healthcare providers
  • Feedback and support requests
  • Survey responses
  • Demo and inquiry requests

Our Commitment to Your Health Data

Your health data is encrypted both in transit and at rest. We use industry-leading security measures to protect this sensitive information and only share it with healthcare providers you explicitly authorize.

2. How We Use Your Information

2.1 Core Functionality

  • Provide real-time and continuous pain assessment
  • Process and display health data from medical devices
  • Enable communication between patients and healthcare providers
  • Generate health insights and biomarker analysis
  • Synchronize data across devices
  • Facilitate authorized data sharing

2.2 Product Improvement

  • Personalize your experience based on user type and preferences
  • Improve our pain assessment algorithms
  • Enhance app and website performance
  • Identify and fix technical issues
  • Monitor data quality and device performance

2.3 Healthcare Coordination

  • Enable healthcare providers to monitor patient health status
  • Facilitate medical consultations and follow-ups
  • Support clinical decision-making
  • Maintain medical records as required by law

2.4 Communication

  • Respond to your inquiries and support requests
  • Send service-related notifications
  • Provide updates about our Services (with your consent)
  • Send marketing communications (with your consent)

2.5 Legal and Safety

  • Comply with applicable laws and regulations
  • Enforce our Terms of Service
  • Protect against fraud, abuse, and security threats
  • Respond to legal requests from authorities

3. How We Share Your Information

3.1 With Healthcare Providers

If you are a patient, your health data is shared with physicians and healthcare administrators you explicitly authorize through our connection features. This sharing is essential for receiving medical care.

3.2 Service Providers

We may share your information with trusted third-party service providers who assist us in:

  • Cloud storage and data hosting
  • Data processing and analysis
  • Technical infrastructure and security
  • Customer support services
  • Payment processing

These service providers are contractually obligated to protect your information and may only use it to provide services on our behalf.

3.3 Business Transfers

If CereVu Medical is involved in a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, your information may be transferred. We will provide notice before your information becomes subject to a different privacy policy.

3.4 Legal Requirements

We may disclose your information if required by law or to:

  • Comply with legal process or government requests
  • Protect the rights, property, or safety of CereVu Medical, our users, or others
  • Investigate fraud, security issues, or technical problems
  • Enforce our agreements and policies

3.5 Aggregate and De-Identified Data

We may share aggregate, de-identified, or anonymized data that cannot reasonably be used to identify you for research, analytics, or business purposes.

We Do NOT

  • Sell your personal information
  • Display third-party advertisements
  • Share your data without authorization (except as required by law)
  • Track you across third-party websites or apps

4. Data Storage and Security

4.1 Data Storage

  • Your data is stored securely on our servers in the United States
  • Health data is encrypted both in transit and at rest
  • We use industry-standard security protocols
  • Mobile app data may be synchronized via iCloud (subject to Apple's privacy policy)

4.2 Security Measures

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your information:

  • Encryption of sensitive data
  • Secure authentication and access controls
  • Regular security assessments and audits
  • Employee training on data protection
  • Monitoring for unauthorized access
  • Incident response procedures

However, no method of transmission over the Internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. While we strive to protect your information, we cannot guarantee absolute security.

4.3 Data Retention

We retain your information for as long as necessary to:

  • Provide you with our Services
  • Comply with legal obligations (including medical record retention requirements)
  • Resolve disputes and enforce our agreements
  • Support business operations and analytics

When you delete your account, we will delete or de-identify your personal information within a reasonable timeframe, except where we must retain it for legal, regulatory, or security purposes.

5. Your Rights and Choices

5.1 Access and Update

You can access and update your personal information through your account settings or by contacting us.

5.2 Delete Your Account

You may request deletion of your account by contacting us at privacy@cerevu.com. Please note that some information may be retained as required by law or for legitimate business purposes.

5.3 Data Portability

You may request a copy of your data in a machine-readable format by contacting us.

5.4 Manage Connections

You can manage which healthcare providers have access to your health data through the app's connection management features.

5.5 Communication Preferences

You can opt-out of marketing communications by following the unsubscribe instructions in emails or contacting us. You cannot opt-out of service-related communications necessary to provide the Services.

6. Health Information and HIPAA

6.1 HIPAA Compliance

If you are receiving healthcare services in the United States, certain health information collected through our Services may be protected under the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA). In such cases:

  • Your healthcare provider is responsible for HIPAA compliance
  • We may act as a Business Associate to your healthcare provider
  • Your healthcare provider's Notice of Privacy Practices governs how they use your health information

6.2 Patient Rights Under HIPAA

If HIPAA applies, you have rights including:

  • The right to access your health information
  • The right to request corrections to your health information
  • The right to receive an accounting of disclosures
  • The right to request restrictions on uses and disclosures

Contact your healthcare provider for more information about your HIPAA rights.

7. California Privacy Rights (CCPA)

If you are a California resident, you have additional rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA):

  • Right to Know: Request disclosure of personal information we collected
  • Right to Delete: Request deletion of your personal information
  • Right to Opt-Out: We do not sell your personal information
  • Right to Non-Discrimination: We will not discriminate against you for exercising your rights

To exercise these rights, contact us at privacy@cerevu.com.

8. European Privacy Rights (GDPR)

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA), United Kingdom, or Switzerland, you have rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR):

  • Right of access to your personal data
  • Right to rectification of inaccurate data
  • Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten")
  • Right to restrict processing
  • Right to data portability
  • Right to object to processing
  • Right to withdraw consent at any time
  • Right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority

9. Children's Privacy

Our Services are not intended for use by children under the age of 13 (or the applicable age of consent in your jurisdiction). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe we have collected information from a child under 13, please contact us immediately.

10. International Users

Our Services are operated from the United States. If you are accessing the Services from outside the United States, please be aware that your information may be transferred to, stored, and processed in the United States where our servers are located. By using our Services, you consent to the transfer of your information to the United States.

11. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, technology, legal requirements, or other factors. We will notify you of any material changes by:

  • Posting the new Privacy Policy on our website and in our app
  • Updating the "Last Updated" date
  • Sending you a notification via email or through the Services

Your continued use of our Services after any changes indicates your acceptance of the updated Privacy Policy.

12. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We use cookies and similar tracking technologies to enhance your experience on our website. You can control cookies through your browser settings. Note that disabling cookies may affect the functionality of certain features.

13. Do Not Track

Our Services do not respond to Do Not Track (DNT) signals because there is no industry standard for how to respond to such signals. We do not track users across third-party websites or apps.
